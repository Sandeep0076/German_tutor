# Goethe A1 Exam Focus - Reading Section (Lesen)

## Overview

The reading section of the Goethe A1 exam consists of three parts (Teil 1, 2, and 3) with a total of 15 questions.

---

## Teil 1 (Part 1) - Questions 1-5

### Format
- **1 large text** (email/notice/message)
- **5 questions** based on that single text
- Answer format: **Richtig** (Correct) or **Falsch** (False)

### Example Structure
You read one email or message (like the example with Karin's email about the train arrival in Hannover), and then answer 5 true/false questions about the content.

**Example:**
- Question 0 (Beispiel): "Lis Zug kommt aus Hannover." → Richtig / Falsch
- Question 1: "Lis Zug kommt nach halb eins an." → Richtig / Falsch
- Question 2: "Karin wartet den ganzen Vormittag vor der Auskunft." → Richtig / Falsch
- (And so on for questions 3-5)

---

## Teil 2 (Part 2) - Questions 6-10

### Format
- **5 questions** (6-10)
- **Each question has its own 2 different texts/websites** (option **a** or **b**)
- Choose which text (a or b) provides the information needed for that specific question

### Important Structure Details
- **NOT** 2 websites for all 5 questions
- **EACH** of the 5 questions has its own unique pair of websites/texts
- Total: 5 questions × 2 websites each = 10 different websites/texts

### Example Structure
Each question presents a scenario and two different websites. You must choose which website (a or b) has the information you need.

**Example Question 0 (Beispiel):**
- Scenario: "Sie wollen wissen: Regnet es in Deutschland?"
- Option **a**: www.openair.de (Open-Air concert information)
- Option **b**: www.dwd.de (German weather service)
- Answer: Choose **b** (weather service has weather information)

**Example Question 6:**
- Scenario: "Sie möchten mit dem Schiff auf dem Rhein fahren."
- Option **a**: www.schiff-ruedesheim.de (Hotel-Pension "Schiff")
- Option **b**: www.bingen-ruedesheimer.de (Bingen-Rüdesheimer Rheinschiffe)
- Answer: Choose **b** (Rhine ships, not a hotel)

**Example Question 7:**
- Scenario: "Sie suchen einen Computerkurs."
- Option **a**: www.vhs-berlin.de (Adult education center with courses)
- Option **b**: www.computer-shop.de (Computer store)
- Answer: Choose **a** (VHS offers courses)

*Note: Questions 8, 9, and 10 would each have their own different pair of websites.*

---

## Teil 3 (Part 3) - Questions 11-15

### Format
- **5 questions** (11-15)
- **Each question has 1 statement + 1 notice/sign/message**
- Answer format: **Richtig** (Correct) or **Falsch** (False)

### Important Structure Details
- Each question is independent with its own notice/sign
- The notice is usually substantial (not just one sentence)
- Common notice types: signs, emails, message boards, announcements, information boards
- You read the notice and decide if the statement is true or false based on the information

### Example Structure
Each question has: (1) A statement to evaluate, and (2) A notice/sign to read

## Summary

| Part | Questions | Format | Answer Type |
|------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Teil 1 | 1-5 (5 questions) | **1 large text** → 5 questions about it | Richtig/Falsch |
| Teil 2 | 6-10 (5 questions) | **Each question** has 2 different texts (a or b) | a or b |
| Teil 3 | 11-15 (5 questions) | **Each question** has 1 statement + 1 notice/sign | Richtig/Falsch |

**Total: 15 questions**

### Key Differences Between Parts:

**Teil 1**: One shared text for all 5 questions
- You read ONE email/message/notice
- Then answer 5 true/false questions about that same text

**Teil 2**: Each question has its own pair of texts
- Question 6 has websites a & b (specific to Q6)
- Question 7 has websites a & b (different from Q6)
- Question 8 has websites a & b (different from Q6 & Q7)
- And so on... (5 questions × 2 texts = 10 different texts total)

**Teil 3**: Each question has its own notice
- Question 11 has statement + notice #1
- Question 12 has statement + notice #2
- Question 13 has statement + notice #3
- And so on... (5 different notices total)

# Goethe A1 Exam Focus - Writing Section (Schreiben)

## Overview

The writing section of the Goethe A1 exam consists of two parts (Teil 1 and 2). This section tests your ability to fill in forms and write short messages in German.

---

## Teil 1 (Part 1) - Form Completion

### Format
- **1 context scenario** (short text describing a situation)
- **1 registration form (Anmeldung)** with missing information
- **5-6 numbered blanks** to fill in (numbered 0-5 or similar)
- Write answers on the **Antwortbogen** (answer sheet)

### Task Description
You read a short context about someone (e.g., a person booking a tour, registering for a course) and help them complete a registration form by filling in the missing information based on the context provided.

### Common Form Types
- Course registration (Sprachschule, Deutschkurs)
- Tour/activity booking (Rundfahrt, Ausflug)
- Hotel/accommodation booking
- Event registration

### Information Typically Required
- **Familienname, Vorname** (Last name, First name)
- **Anzahl der Personen** (Number of people)
- **Davon Kinder** (Of which children)
- **Adresse** (Address): Straße, Hausnummer, PLZ, Urlaubsort
- **Geburtsdatum** (Date of birth)
- **Geburtsort** (Place of birth)
- **Muttersprache** (Mother tongue)
- **Andere Sprachen** (Other languages)
- **Zahlungsweise** (Payment method): Bar, Kreditkarte
- **Reisetermin/Kurstermin** (Travel/course date)
- **Kurszeit** (Course time)
- **Unterschrift** (Signature)

### Example Structure

**Context (Teil 1):**
```
Ihre Freundin, Eva Kadavy, macht mit ihrem Mann und ihren beiden 
Söhnen (8 und 11 Jahre alt) Urlaub in Seeheim. Im Reisebüro bucht 
sie für den nächsten Sonntag eine Busfahrt um den Bodensee. 
Frau Kadavy hat keine Kreditkarte.

In dem Formular fehlen fünf Informationen.
Helfen Sie Ihrer Freundin und schreiben Sie die fünf fehlenden 
Informationen in das Formular.
Am Ende schreiben Sie Ihre Lösungen bitte auf den Antwortbogen.
```

**Form to Complete:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│        BODENSEE-RUNDFAHRT                       │
│              Anmeldung                          │
│                                                 │
│ Familienname, Vorname:  Kadavy, Eva        (0) │
│ Anzahl der Personen:    _______________    (1) │
│ Davon Kinder:           _______________    (2) │
│ Urlaubsadresse:         Hotel Schönblick       │
│ Straße, Hausnummer.:    Burgstraße 34          │
│ PLZ, Urlaubsort:        78014 __________   (3) │
│                                                 │
│ Der Reisepreis ist mit der Anmeldung zu         │
│ bezahlen.                                       │
│ Zahlungsweise:                             (4) │
│   ☐ Bar                                         │
│   ☐ Kreditkarte                                 │
│                                                 │
│ Reisetermin:            _______________    (5) │
│                                                 │
│ Unterschrift:           Eva Kadavy              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Answers:**
- (1) 4 (Anzahl der Personen: Eva, Mann, 2 Söhne)
- (2) 2 (Davon Kinder: 2 Söhne)
- (3) Seeheim (PLZ, Urlaubsort)
- (4) Bar (Zahlungsweise - text says "keine Kreditkarte")
- (5) Sonntag / nächsten Sonntag (Reisetermin)

### Tips for Teil 1
- Read the context carefully to find all required information
- Some information is already filled in as examples (marked with 0)
- Look for key words: numbers, dates, places, names
- Pay attention to details like "keine Kreditkarte" (no credit card)
- Write clearly on the answer sheet
- Check that your answers match the form fields

---

## Teil 2 (Part 2) - Short Message/Email Writing

### Format
- **1 scenario/situation** requiring you to write a message
- **3 content points** you must address in your message
- Write **1-2 sentences per point** (approximately 30 words total)
- Include **Anrede** (greeting) and **Gruß** (closing)

### Task Description
You write a short message or email to someone (e.g., tourist information, a friend, a company) addressing three specific points given in the prompt.

### Common Message Types
- Email to tourist information (Touristeninformation)
- Message to a friend
- Inquiry to a company/organization
- Request for information
- Booking confirmation

### Structure Requirements
Your message must include:
1. **Anrede** (Greeting): "Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren," / "Liebe/r..."
2. **Content**: Address all 3 points (1-2 sentences each)
3. **Gruß** (Closing): "Mit freundlichen Grüßen" / "Viele Grüße"

### Example Structure

**Scenario (Teil 2):**
```
Sie möchten im August Dresden besuchen. Schreiben Sie an die 
Touristeninformation:

- Warum schreiben Sie?
- Bitten Sie: Informationen über Filme, Museen usw. (Kulturprogramm).
- Fragen Sie: Hoteladressen?

Schreiben Sie zu jedem Punkt ein bis zwei Sätze auf den 
Antwortbogen (circa 30 Wörter).
Schreiben Sie auch eine Anrede und einen Gruß.
```

**Sample Answer:**
```
Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren,

ich möchte im August Dresden besuchen. Können Sie mir bitte 
Informationen über das Kulturprogramm schicken? Ich interessiere 
mich für Filme und Museen. Haben Sie auch Hoteladressen für mich?

Mit freundlichen Grüßen
[Your name]
```

### Content Points Breakdown

**Point 1: Warum schreiben Sie?** (Why are you writing?)
- "Ich möchte im August Dresden besuchen."
- State your purpose/reason for writing

**Point 2: Bitten Sie: Informationen über...** (Request: Information about...)
- "Können Sie mir bitte Informationen über das Kulturprogramm schicken?"
- "Ich interessiere mich für Filme und Museen."
- Make a polite request for specific information

**Point 3: Fragen Sie: Hoteladressen?** (Ask: Hotel addresses?)
- "Haben Sie auch Hoteladressen für mich?"
- Ask a specific question

### Common Phrases for Teil 2

**Greetings (Anrede):**
- Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren, (formal)
- Liebe/r [Name], (informal, to friends)
- Hallo [Name], (casual)

**Stating Purpose:**
- Ich möchte... (I would like to...)
- Ich schreibe, weil... (I'm writing because...)
- Ich habe eine Frage zu... (I have a question about...)

**Making Requests:**
- Können Sie mir bitte... schicken? (Can you please send me...?)
- Ich brauche Informationen über... (I need information about...)
- Bitte schicken Sie mir... (Please send me...)

**Asking Questions:**
- Haben Sie...? (Do you have...?)
- Wann ist...? (When is...?)
- Wo kann ich...? (Where can I...?)
- Wie viel kostet...? (How much does... cost?)

**Closings (Gruß):**
- Mit freundlichen Grüßen (formal)
- Viele Grüße (friendly)
- Liebe Grüße (warm, to friends)

### Tips for Teil 2
- Address ALL 3 points clearly
- Keep it simple and direct (A1 level)
- Use polite forms: "Können Sie bitte...?" "Ich möchte..."
- Don't forget greeting and closing
- Approximately 30 words total (don't count greeting/closing)
- Write 1-2 sentences per point
- Check spelling of common words
- Use connectors: und, aber, oder

---

## Summary - Writing Section

| Part | Task | Format | Key Requirements |
|------|------|--------|------------------|
| **Teil 1** | Form completion | Read context + fill in 5-6 blanks in a form | Extract information from text; write on answer sheet |
| **Teil 2** | Short message/email | Write message addressing 3 points | Include greeting, 3 content points (1-2 sentences each), closing (~30 words) |

### Key Differences Between Parts:

**Teil 1**: Information extraction and form filling
- Read a scenario/context
- Find specific information (names, dates, numbers, places)
- Fill in missing form fields
- Focus: accuracy and attention to detail

**Teil 2**: Creative writing (guided)
- Write a short message/email
- Address 3 specific points given in the prompt
- Use appropriate greetings and closings
- Focus: communication and basic sentence structure

### Overall Writing Section Tips:
- **Teil 1**: Read the context twice; underline key information
- **Teil 2**: Plan your 3 points before writing; keep sentences simple
- Write clearly and legibly on the answer sheet
- Manage your time: don't spend too long on one part
- Check your work for basic spelling and grammar
- Use vocabulary and structures you know well (A1 level)

**Total Questions: 15**
- **Teil 1**: 5 questions (Richtig/Falsch) - One long email about library services
- **Teil 2**: 5 questions (a or b) - Each question with 2 different websites
- **Teil 3**: 5 questions (Richtig/Falsch) - Each question with its own notice/sign

**Theme**: Public Library & Books
**Difficulty Level**: A1 (with some challenging vocabulary appropriate for exam preparation)
**Real-world relevance**: Library visits, memberships, rules, and services

**Key Vocabulary Practiced**:
- Library services (ausleihen, zurückgeben, Leihfrist, Bibliothekskarte)
- Opening hours (Öffnungszeiten, geschlossen, geöffnet)
- Rules and regulations (Regeln, erlaubt, verboten)
- Time expressions (täglich, wöchentlich, Wochenende)
- Numbers and prices (Euro, kostenlos, Gebühr)

**Tips for Students**:
1. **Teil 1**: Read the email twice, underline key dates, times, and numbers
2. **Teil 2**: Focus on what each website offers - match the need with the service
3. **Teil 3**: Pay attention to words like "kein", "nicht", "verboten" that indicate negatives
4. Look for exact information - don't assume or guess
5. Practice time management - don't spend too long on one question
